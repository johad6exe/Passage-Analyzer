The boy had been watching the same stars for three nights now, somewhere between the oasis and the pyramids. Santiago knew the desert spoke its own language — not in words, but in omens. The old alchemist had told him that the Soul of the World whispered to those willing to listen, that every grain of sand held a secret written by the hand of God. He thought of Fatima, her dark eyes patient as the dunes, and pressed his hand against the warm earth. His Personal Legend was close. He could feel it the way a falcon feels the wind — not by thinking, but by simply being alive. The treasure was never really buried gold. It was this: the long walk itself, the fear overcome, the love found and left behind. Maktub, the old man had said. It is written.
